Pillar One

Educate the Young

An auditorium built at Uravakonda Jr College now hosts everything from morning prayers to final exams. Computers and exam planks delivered to GVMC High School, Vizag. Fifty children sponsored every year with uniforms and supplies. Education needs infrastructure. We build it, stock it, and stay.

Our education work covers three areas: infrastructure (auditoriums, classrooms, computer labs), supplies (uniforms, books, exam materials, bags), and STEM access (computers, digital equipment) for government schools that lack resources.

Pillar Two

Care for Every Age

Growing old — or growing up with no one in your corner — should not mean growing invisible. We bring care to seniors and youth alike: quarterly drives, essentials, companionship, and the consistent attention that turns a visit into a commitment, not a courtesy.

We partner with elder care homes and community centers to provide both material support and human presence, because what many people need most — young or old — isn't supplies. It's someone who shows up.

Pillar Three

Build What Lasts

Programs end. Buildings, water plants, and supply lines don't. An auditorium at Uravakonda, computer labs at GVMC High School, water filtration for flood-hit communities — pillar three is why pillars one and two survive us.

Our infrastructure work covers three areas: permanent spaces (auditoriums, classrooms, computer labs), utilities (water filtration plants, sanitation), and supply logistics (flood relief kits, supply drives across 19+ schools) that keep Education and Care running long after any single event ends.

Flagship Initiative

Project Parivarthan

In a junior college classroom in Kurnool, a sixteen-year-old raises his hand and asks a question he's never said out loud: how do I stop being on my phone at 2 a.m.? That question, honest, uncomfortable, and urgent, is why Project Parivarthan exists.

In partnership with the Government of India, our mental health professionals run workshops and clinics on substance abuse, screen addiction, peer pressure, and emotional well-being across 8 districts of Andhra Pradesh. Next: taking Parivarthan beyond Andhra Pradesh, so that the question asked in Kurnool gets answered everywhere it's being silently held.
